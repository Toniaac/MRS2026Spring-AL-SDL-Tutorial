## Overview

Modern materials research is increasingly constrained not by ideas, but by data. High-quality experimental datasets are expensive, slow to generate, and often limited by synthesis and characterization throughput and human error. As a result, many materials problems operate firmly in the low-data regime, where traditional trial-and-error or grid-based experimentation becomes inefficient and difficult to scale. Recent advances in automation and high-throughput experimentation have enabled closed-loop, self-driving laboratory workflows, creating a growing need for algorithms that can guide experiments efficiently. This tutorial introduces core concepts and practical intuition behind machine learning and active learning in the context of materials science, with a focus on data-efficient experimental exploration. The tutorial will also introduce emerging approaches that leverage large language models for experimental exploration and decision-making, including training-free or weakly supervised strategies that operate with minimal numerical data or explicit objective functions. Attendees will have the option to work through guided code notebooks that demonstrate end-to-end workflows, with a focus on practical usage and decision-making rather than algorithmic detail. A live demonstration will show how active learning algorithms interface with a self-driving laboratory in practice. This tutorial is intended for materials scientists seeking hands-on exposure to machine-learning-guided experimentation and a practical understanding of how active learning and self-driving laboratories can be combined to accelerate experimental iteration.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this tutorial, participants should be able to:

- Understand the role of machine learning and active learning in materials science 
- Apply machine learning and active learning to guide experimental decisions 
- Evaluate the role and limitations of large language model-guided active learning 
- Integrate active learning with a self-driving laboratory workflow
